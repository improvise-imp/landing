Privacy Policy

Improvise · Last updated: May 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Improvise (“we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use the Improvise mobile application and related services (the “App”), including our website at improviseapp.net.

By creating an account or using the App, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1. Information we collect

Account information

When you register or sign in, we collect:

  • Email address (for email/password accounts)
  • Display name (shown in the App and, if applicable, on leaderboards)
  • Authentication data managed by our auth provider (e.g. sign-in method, session tokens)

If you sign in with Apple or Google, we receive information those providers share with us according to your choices on their sign-in screens (typically email and name).

Training and progress data

To operate the App, we store data such as:

  • Experience points (XP), rank, streaks, and completed scenarios
  • Daily challenge progress, achievements, and scenario attempt history
  • Attribute scores and related training statistics
  • Referral codes, referral activity, and premium/reward status
  • Subscription and entitlement status (via our subscription partner)
  • Real Life Mode usage counts and saved analyses (where you use that feature)

Journal and Real Life content

If you use Real Life Mode or the Journal, we store:

  • Text you submit (situations, journal entries, optional titles)
  • Optional moods, tags, and metadata you choose
  • AI-generated insights we produce when you request analysis
  • Timestamps and sync identifiers needed to save and restore your entries

Do not submit information you are not comfortable storing and processing as described in this policy.

Device and technical data

We may collect or generate:

  • App version, platform (iOS/Android), and coarse usage events for product analytics (see Section 4)
  • Local caches on your device (profile, progress, drafts) for offline use and performance
  • Push notification tokens if you enable notifications (handled by your device OS and our notification services)

We do not intentionally collect precise location, contacts, photos, or microphone data for core App features.

2. How we use your information

We use your information to:

  • Create and secure your account and authenticate you across devices
  • Save and sync progress, journal entries, and Real Life analyses
  • Deliver scenarios, daily challenges, leaderboards, and referrals
  • Process subscriptions and premium features (Improvise Pro)
  • Generate AI insights when you request analysis in Real Life Mode or the Journal
  • Improve reliability, fix bugs, and understand how features are used (analytics)
  • Respond to support requests and enforce our Terms

We do not sell your personal information.

3. Where data is stored

Account, progress, journal entries, and related cloud data are stored on secure infrastructure operated by Supabase (hosting and database). AI analysis requests are processed on our backend before calling third-party AI APIs.

A copy of your profile and progress may be cached on your device (e.g. via local storage) so the App can load faster and work briefly offline. Auth session data remains on your device until you sign out.

Unsaved journal drafts may remain on your device until you save an entry to your account or clear App data.

4. AI and third-party services

We use trusted service providers to run the App. They process data only as needed to provide their services to us:

Provider Purpose Typical data
Supabase Authentication, database, cloud sync, server functions Account, profile, progress, journal entries, server-side logic
Anthropic AI analysis for Real Life Mode and Journal (when you request it) Text you submit for analysis (not used for unrelated training by us beyond providing the feature)
RevenueCat Subscriptions and entitlements (Improvise Pro) on iOS and Android Purchase/entitlement identifiers linked to your account
Apple / Google Sign-in and in-app purchases through their stores Per their policies when you use Sign in with Apple/Google or subscribe
PostHog Product analytics (how features are used) Screen paths, feature events, coarse metadata (e.g. completion flags, error categories)—not journal body text, Real Life situations, email, or passwords

Product analytics (PostHog)

We use PostHog to understand usage—for example sign-up completion, scenario completion, paywall views, and sync health—so we can improve the App. Analytics events are designed to avoid sensitive content: we use length bands and categories rather than full text of journal or Real Life submissions. When analytics is enabled in production builds, events may be associated with your authenticated user ID in PostHog for funnel and retention analysis.

Each provider processes data under its own terms and privacy policy. We encourage you to review those policies.

5. What other users can see

If you appear on the leaderboard, other signed-in users may see your display name, XP, and streak (including weekly leaderboard views where applicable).

We do not show your email address, journal entries, or Real Life analyses to other users. Choose a display name you are comfortable sharing publicly on leaderboards.

We may disclose information if required by law, to protect rights and safety, or in connection with a merger or asset sale, subject to applicable law.

6. Your choices and rights

  • Display name: Update in App Settings.
  • Notifications: Manage in your device system settings.
  • Subscriptions: Manage, cancel, or restore via the App Store or Google Play (and Improvise Pro settings in the App).
  • Sign out: Ends your session on the device; cloud data remains until you delete your account.
  • Delete account: Available in App Settings. This requests deletion of your cloud profile and associated data via our deletion process. Clearing App data alone may remove local caches but not server data.

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict certain processing of your personal data. Contact us (Section 10) to make a request. We may need to verify your identity.

7. Data retention

We retain your information while your account is active and as needed to provide the App, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. When you delete your account, we delete or anonymize cloud profile data in line with our deletion procedures, except where retention is required by law or legitimate business needs (e.g. fraud prevention, billing records held by app stores).

8. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your information, including encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS) and access controls on our cloud infrastructure. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Children

The App is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your country). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us data, contact us and we will take appropriate steps to delete it.

10. International users

If you use the App from outside the country where our servers or providers operate, your information may be processed in the United States or other locations where our service providers maintain facilities. By using the App, you understand your information may be transferred to those locations, which may have different data protection rules than your country.
